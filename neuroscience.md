# The Grand Picture of a Computational Neuroscientist

Neuro System involves many scales. It is in the core of philosophy and human knowledge to inquire how the **intelligence**, how our **consciousness**, how man come out of the biophysical cell assembly that we think is strongly governed by physics laws. To find out the **restrictions our biological brain posed on us mind** is related to our everyday life. And using devices to **interact with, to repair or reconstruct** the neural system is the final application we want to realize.  

Inspired by the **Condensed Matter Physics** Research, many accept the idea that "more is different", so some use the word "emergent" to call the difference from the discribable behavior of few neuron and the myriad interacting neurons in a complex network constructing the immensely-complex brain. But terming this difference is not explaining it. We still don't know on what scale the transition happened. So there are theoretical and simulation efforts on large scale neural networks, in order to see what behavior may interacting units in large number generate. This can lead to research using perturbations method in statistical mechanics and quantum field theory to the neurons. 

Incited by the **Machine Learning** and Artificial Neural Network research, we find machines utilizing trained Neural Network structures can achieve likewise results as human subjects. Just as we can learn to categrize objects, to find patterns in words from examples and labels, the machines can do so as well (Supervised learning). Just as human and animals can learn behavior sequence or task strategy from trial and error,  the machines can do so as well (Reinforcement learning). While the speed of learning, amount of data needed for learning differs greatly in human and machines. But whether the neural system achieve the result in the same manner as human beings is still in question. 

Some mathematicians motivated by aesthetics, believing that so powerful and complex a system must operates with beautiful **mathematical** rules just like the Universe in the physicists' mind. So they apply advanced mathematical tools on neural system: Complex Network Theory can be applied on the neural netwrok architecture. Topology on the network structure or on the mapping between stimulus and neural response. Information theory on the information transfer problem in neural network. Dynamical system approach to analyze various dynamical behavior on subject's perception or behavior side or on the neurons' side. Riemman geometry or other differential geometry approach on visual system. 

Due to the Availability of Data, there are extensive researchs on the Input/Output systems of brain, i.e. the **sensation and motor** systems, both on the Psychophysical side or on the Neural Circuit side. How the neural system process natural stimuli, how the information finally transformed into perception and comprehension is not known. Building neural network models to explain or fit behavioral data and electrophysiology data is not that difficult. But in this paradigm what biological constraint to use and what principles or new phenomena to draw from model is more tricky. 

Despite the grand theoretical and philosophical motivation, scientific research is largely restricted by the Analytical Methods, Computation Techniques of theorists and Available Data from experimentalists. **For a theorist**, what he can contribute to the picture is bearing models in mind to look deeper into data, and to find intrinsic generalities that experimentalist working on one system or using one approach cannot see. And I've learnt from my experience that theorists have to work closely with experimentalists to get intuition, to refine and to test their models. 

We are still very far from the answer.
